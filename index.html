Mikki About

Mikki Janower is a designer and art director working across editorial layout, interaction design, branding, and typography. She is currently based in Brooklyn, where she maintains an active freelance practice and cultivates her small but growing library of magazines.

She recently recieved a BFA in Communication Design from Washington University in St. Louis and is on the lookout for postgraduate opportunities.

She's open to new projects, collaborations, conversations, and friends! Track her down via email, Instagram, or Are.na. You can also download her resume, find her on LinkedIn, or send a nice note to her mother.

Cyberotica
On art, sex, & robots
Cyberotica compiles recent works by over fifty artists—including Jacolby Satterwhite, Hito Steyerl, and Marguerite Humeau—to explore a prominent motif in the contemporary oeuvre: the chimera of fetish and technology.

Spanning gender politics, decolonization, and techno-utopianism, Cyberotica presents a vast narrative of identity and protest.
2020
Postcursor / Starrynite
Machine learning as conditional design
Postcursor and Starrynite are microsites trained to generate graphics according to users’ hand movements, exploring the notion of process-as-product.

Each site uses the same machine learning model to its own end. Postcursor lets users draw cursors across the screen, while Starrynite leaves a trail of stars across the user's backdrop.
2020
Reflections on Practice
Not a process book
Reflections on Practice documents and discusses my creative output over the course of six months at Washington University in St. Louis.

Each book synthesizes traditional methods of process documentation with relevant essays and ruminations. The result is an intimate but robust reflection on learning in the institutional sphere.

Cocktails of the Major Arcana

Cocktails of the Major Arcana is a collection of cocktail recipes inspired by the art of tarot.

Each hand-drawn illustration represents one character in the tarot pantheon. On the backside of each card, a curated cocktail represents the corresponding character.

Reconna Typeface
A typeface inspired by 19th-century print
Reconna is a prototype inspired letterforms by found in 20th-century Italian print archives. The condensed serif has high ascenders and angled serifs.

Glyph set includes numerals, basic punctuation, and a variety of symbols.
